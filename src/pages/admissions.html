Entry Requirements

To ensure the best learning experience, we have specific entry requirements for each of our programmes. Below are the detailed requirements for each programme:

Diploma in Developing Leaders (MQF Level 5)

Academic Requirements

MQF Level 4 qualification or equivalent, or relevant work experience in the field.

Language Proficiency

Proficiency in English (both spoken and written) is required.

Additional Requirements

Minimum age: 21 years
Prior work or volunteer experience is preferred.

Award in Basic Courier Service (MQF Level 1)

Essential Requirements

Valid driving or riding licence.

Language and Skills

Basic English literacy and digital skills.

Additional Information

No prior qualifications required.
Ideal for individuals looking to start work in delivery or courier roles.

Award in Foundations of English for Foreigners (MQF Level 1)

General Requirements

No previous English qualification required.

Age Requirement

Minimum age: 16 years.

Recommended Skills

Basic literacy skills preferred.

Award in Foundations of Maltese for Foreigners (MQF Level 1)

General Requirements

No prior knowledge of Maltese required.

Age Requirement

Minimum age: 16 years.

Additional Information

Willingness to participate in class and practice speaking is essential.

Important Notes:

  • All applicants may be required to attend an interview or assessment.
  • Additional documentation may be requested during the application process.
  • Meeting the minimum requirements does not guarantee admission.
  • Special consideration may be given to mature students with relevant experience.

How to Apply

Follow these simple steps to apply for your chosen programme at Advanced Learning:

Step 1

Choose Your Programme

Browse our programmes page and select the course that matches your interests and career goals.

Step 2

Check Entry Requirements

Ensure you meet the academic and language requirements for your chosen programme.

Step 3

Prepare Your Documents

Gather all required documents as listed in the document checklist section below.

Step 4

Submit Your Application

Complete the online application form and upload all required documents. Alternatively, you can visit our campus to submit your application in person.

Step 5

Application Review

Our admissions team will review your application and may contact you for additional information or an interview.

Step 6

Receive Admission Decision

You will be notified of the admission decision via email within 10-15 working days after submitting a complete application.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our admissions process below.

What are the language requirements for international students?

International students whose first language is not English must demonstrate English language proficiency. We accept IELTS (minimum 5.5 overall), TOEFL iBT (minimum 70), or equivalent qualifications. Some programmes may have higher language requirements.

Can I apply for more than one programme?

Yes, you can apply for up to three programmes. Please submit a separate application for each programme and indicate your order of preference. Each application requires its own set of supporting documents.

Is there an application fee?

Yes, there is a non-refundable application fee of €50 for all programmes. This fee covers the administrative costs of processing your application. The fee can be paid online via credit/debit card or through bank transfer.

How long does the admission process take?

Once we receive your complete application with all required documents, the review process typically takes 10-15 working days. During peak periods, it may take longer. You will be notified via email once a decision has been made on your application.

What are the tuition fees and payment options?

Tuition fees vary by programme. Detailed fee information can be found on each programme page. We offer several payment options including full payment at the beginning of the programme or installment plans. Some scholarships and financial aid options may be available for eligible students.

Can I defer my admission to a later intake?

Yes, in most cases you can defer your admission to the next available intake. Requests for deferral must be made in writing and are subject to approval. Deferral is typically granted for up to one academic year. Please contact our admissions office for more information.

What support services are available for international students?

We offer comprehensive support services for international students including airport pickup, accommodation assistance, orientation programmes, academic advising, career counseling, and 24/7 emergency support. Our international student office is available to help with visa applications, health insurance, and any other concerns you may have.
